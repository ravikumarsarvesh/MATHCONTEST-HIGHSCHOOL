Part A: Each correct answer is worth 5

Q1. The value of \(2 \times 2015 - 2015\) is
(A) 2015   (B) 4030   (C) 6045   (D) 0   (E) −2015
Q2. The expression \(\sqrt{1}+\sqrt{9}\) is equal to
(A) 1   (B) 2   (C) 3   (D) 4   (E) 5
Q3. A rectangular box has a base that measures 2 cm by 5 cm and a volume of 30 cm³. The height of the box is
(A) 6 cm   (B) 5 cm   (C) 3 cm   (D) 4 cm   (E) 2 cm
Q4. In the diagram, \(R\) is on line segment \(PS\). Also, \(\angle RPQ = 50°\) and \(\angle PQR = 90°\). The value of \(x\) is
(A) 50   (B) 90   (C) 130   (D) 140   (E) 150
Q5. From the given graph, the probability that a randomly chosen province/territory joined Confederation between 1890 and 1969 is
(A) \(\frac{3}{13}\)   (B) \(\frac{4}{13}\)   (C) \(\frac{5}{13}\)   (D) \(\frac{6}{13}\)   (E) \(\frac{7}{13}\)
Q6. If \(a^2 = 9\), the value of \(a^4\) is
(A) 3   (B) 81   (C) 18   (D) 27   (E) 36
Q7. Which of the following is NOT equal to \(3 + \frac{1}{10} + \frac{4}{100}\)?
(A) 3.14   (B) \(3\frac{14}{100}\)   (C) \(3\frac{5}{110}\)   (D) \(\frac{157}{50}\)   (E) \(3\frac{7}{50}\)
Q8. Violet has half the money needed for a necklace. Her sister gives her $30, bringing her to three-quarters. Her father gives the rest. How much does her father give?
(A) $7.50   (B) $15   (C) $22.50   (D) $30   (E) $120
Q9. January 5 is a Monday. John goes for a run every 3 days starting January 5. The first Monday after January 5 on which John goes for a run is
(A) January 12   (B) January 19   (C) January 26   (D) February 2   (E) February 9
Q10. In the diagram, square \(PQRS\) has side length 3. Points \(T\), \(U\) divide \(QR\) into thirds and \(V\), \(W\) divide \(RS\) into thirds. The ratio of shaded area to unshaded area is
(A) 2:1   (B) 5:4   (C) 3:2   (D) 7:2   (E) 4:1

Part B: Each correct answer is worth 6

Q11. The value of \(4 \otimes 8\) where \(a \otimes b = \frac{a}{b} + \frac{b}{a}\) is
(A) 2   (B) \(\frac{1}{2}\)   (C) 4   (D) \(\frac{3}{2}\)   (E) \(\frac{5}{2}\)
Q12. The line segment joining \((-1,q)\) and \((-3,r)\) is parallel to \(y = \frac{3}{2}x + 1\). The value of \(r - q\) is
(A) 3   (B) −6   (C) 6   (D) −6   (E) −3
Q13. 36 students play baseball, hockey, or both. 25 play baseball, 19 play hockey. How many play both?
(A) 7   (B) 8   (C) 9   (D) 10   (E) 11
Q14. \(\triangle PQR\) has perimeter 22 with \(PS = SR = x\) and \(PQ = PR\). The perimeter of \(PQRS\) is 24. The value of \(x\) is
(A) 4   (B) 5   (C) 7   (D) 6   (E) 8
Q15. The ones digit of \(1! + 2! + 3! + \cdots + 10!\) is
(A) 0   (B) 3   (C) 5   (D) 7   (E) 9
Q16. In a magic square with entries \(a, 13, b\) (row 1), \(19, c, 11\) (row 2), \(12, ?, 16\) (row 3), the value of \(a + b + c\) is
(A) 40   (B) 44   (C) 47   (D) 50   (E) 53
Q17. Deanna drove 100 km in one hour. In the first 30 min she drove at \(v\) km/h, in the second 30 min at \((v+20)\) km/h. The value of \(v\) is
(A) 80   (B) 90   (C) 100   (D) 110   (E) 120
Q18. A semicircle has diameter 20. Rectangle \(PQRS\) is inscribed with \(PQ\) on the diameter and \(PQ = 16\). The length of \(PS\) is
(A) 6   (B) 7   (C) 8   (D) 9   (E) 10
Q19. A bank teller makes stacks of $1000 using $20 and $50 bills (at least one of each). The maximum number of stacks is
(A) 9   (B) 10   (C) 11   (D) 12   (E) 13
Q20. For how many integers \(n\) with \(-3 \leq n \leq 4\) is \(72\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^n\) an integer?
(A) 2   (B) 3   (C) 4   (D) 5   (E) 6

Part C: Each correct answer is worth 8

Q21. Three consecutive odd integers have average 7. A positive integer \(m\) (different from the three) is included so the average of all four is an integer. The sum of the three smallest possible values of \(m\) is
(A) 21   (B) 25   (C) 27   (D) 29   (E) 31
Q22. Six players each play 10 games (2 against each other). Win = 1 pt, tie = ½ pt, loss = 0. The minimum points to guarantee having more than every other player is
(A) 8   (B) \(8\frac{1}{2}\)   (C) 9   (D) \(9\frac{1}{2}\)   (E) 10
Q23. Nylah's lights come on randomly at 7:00, 7:30, 8:00, 8:30, or 9:00 p.m. (each with probability \(\frac{1}{5}\)) and go off at a random time between 11:00 p.m. and 1:00 a.m. The probability the lights are on for between 4 and 5 hours is
(A) \(\frac{1}{4}\)   (B) \(\frac{3}{10}\)   (C) \(\frac{1}{3}\)   (D) \(\frac{3}{8}\)   (E) \(\frac{7}{20}\)
Q24. A \(6 \times 4\) region is tiled with \(1 \times 2\) tiles. A line divides it into a \(2 \times 4\) and \(4 \times 4\) region with no tile crossing the line. The number of tilings is
(A) 180   (B) 160   (C) 144   (D) 120   (E) 100
Q25. A square-based prism has base side \(a\) and height \(h\). Point \(X\) inside has \(PX = 12\), \(QX = 10\), \(RX = 8\). The maximum area of face \(PQUT\) is closest to
(A) 48.00   (B) 67.82   (C) 96.00   (D) 128.00   (E) 28.00
